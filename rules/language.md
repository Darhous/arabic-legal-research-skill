# Language Rules

Generated writing must use formal Arabic academic legal style.

## Required Style

Use language that is:

- Objective.
- Precise.
- Analytical.
- Legally disciplined.
- Clear without oversimplification.
- Connected to the research problem.
- Free from unnecessary rhetorical flourish.

## Avoid

Do not use:

- Excitement style.
- Oratory.
- Imagination.
- Emotional language.
- Sarcasm.
- Mockery.
- Superiority.
- Self-praise or praise of others.
- Absolute categorical wording unless supported.
- Excessive first-person expressions such as `أنا`, `أرى`, `رأيي`, `قلت`.
- Vague or ambiguous expressions.
- Unnecessary verbosity.
- Harmful over-compression where explanation is needed.
- Naming people in criticism unless scientifically necessary.

## Preferred Formulations

Prefer cautious academic formulations such as:

- يتضح من ذلك
- يمكن القول
- يذهب جانب من الفقه
- يستخلص الباحث من العرض السابق
- يثور التساؤل حول
- تبدو أهمية هذه المسألة في

Use these phrases only where substantively appropriate. Do not turn them into repetitive filler.

## Language Failure Conditions

Fail language review if:

- The text uses rhetorical slogans instead of analysis.
- The text attacks people rather than ideas or legal positions.
- The text contains unsupported certainty.
- The text hides unclear reasoning behind ornate language.
- The text relies heavily on first-person claims.
- The writing is so compressed that legal reasoning becomes unclear.
